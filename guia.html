About Sucre

Bolivia's most livable city

Sucre is Bolivia's constitutional capital and one of South America's best-preserved colonial cities. Its whitewashed baroque architecture earned it the nickname La Ciudad Blanca — and UNESCO recognition in 1991.

At 2,810 meters, it sits low enough for easy acclimatization, yet high enough to guarantee cool evenings and the brilliant Andean light that makes every street corner worth photographing.

The city is compact, walkable, and deeply student-oriented — it's home to one of the oldest universities in the Americas (USFX, founded 1624), making it South America's top destination for Spanish immersion.

Practical Info

Plan your Visit

Everything you need to know to navigate the city safely and comfortably.

When to Visit

2,810m — Gentle

Sucre has 'eternal spring' weather. The best time is the dry season (May to October), with clear blue skies every day.

Currency

Boliviano (Bs)

1 USD ≈ 6.91 Bs. ATMs are available in the historic center. USD and euros can be exchanged at casa de cambio. Cards accepted at most restaurants.

Transport

Walk Everything

The historic center is perfectly walkable. For further spots, 'micros' (buses) are an experience themselves. Taxis are cheap and plentiful.

Best Time to Visit

May – October

Dry season. Clear skies, warm days, cool nights. Sucre is pleasant year-round — the rainy season (Nov–Mar) brings afternoon showers but stays green and lush.
