About Sucre

Bolivia's most livable city

Sucre is Bolivia's constitutional capital and one of South America's best-preserved colonial cities. Its whitewashed baroque architecture earned it the nickname La Ciudad Blanca — and UNESCO recognition in 1991.

At 2,810 meters, it sits low enough for easy acclimatization, yet high enough to guarantee cool evenings and the brilliant Andean light that makes every street corner worth photographing.

The city is compact, walkable, and deeply student-oriented — it's home to one of the oldest universities in the Americas (USFX, founded 1624), making it South America's top destination for Spanish immersion.

In the City

Must-See Spots

Most of the historic center is walkable in an hour. Here are the places worth slowing down for.

Start Here Free

Central Square

Plaza 25 de Mayo

The heart of Sucre. Lined with colonial facades, the Cathedral, and shaded benches where locals and travelers mix. The best place to start — and to end — any day.

Museum · Bs 25

Casa de la Libertad

Where Bolivia's independence was declared in 1825. A museum today — don't skip it.

Viewpoint · Free

La Recoleta

Colonial convent on the hill. Best panoramic view of the entire white city.

Architecture · Free to enter

Metropolitan Cathedral

Baroque masterpiece on the main plaza. The bell tower at sunset is unmissable. Small religious museum inside.

Museum · Bs 30

ASUR Textile Museum

Stunning collection of Jalq'a and Tarabuco indigenous weavings. One of Bolivia's finest ethnographic museums.

Day Half · 6km from center

Cal Orcko Dinosaurs

The world's largest collection of dinosaur footprints — 5,000+ tracks on a vertical limestone cliff, 68 million years old.

Before You Arrive

Practical Tips

Altitude

2,810m — Gentle

Far lower than La Paz (3,650m) or Potosí (4,090m). Most travelers feel fine within a day. Drink water, rest your first afternoon, and try coca tea.

Currency

Boliviano (Bs)

1 USD ≈ 6.91 Bs. ATMs are available in the historic center. USD and euros can be exchanged at casa de cambio. Cards accepted at most restaurants.

Getting Around

Walk Everything

The historic center is fully walkable. Taxis anywhere in the center cost 5–15 Bs (flat rate, no meter). Micros (local buses) run to all neighborhoods for 1.50 Bs.

Best Time to Visit

May – October

Dry season. Clear skies, warm days, cool nights. Sucre is pleasant year-round — the rainy season (Nov–Mar) brings afternoon showers but stays green and lush.
